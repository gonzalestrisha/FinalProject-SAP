Rule No.1

Dormitories are shared living spaces, so it's important to be considerate of your neighbors. Observe designated quiet hours, typically from 10 pm to 7 am, to minimize noise and allow others to rest or study.

Rule No.2

Keep your dorm room and common areas clean and tidy. Dispose of trash properly, clean up after yourself, and avoid leaving personal belongings scattered around

Rule No.3

Maintain open communication with your roommates and dormitory staff. Address any concerns or issues directly and respectfully.

Rule No.4

Respect the personal property of your roommates and other residents. Do not borrow items without permission and avoid entering someone else's room without their consent.

Rule No.5

Inform your resident director or dormitory management of any maintenance issues promptly.

Rule No.6

Keep noise levels under control, especially during quiet hours.

Rule No.7

Treat common areas like the kitchen, living room, and laundry room with respect.

Rule No.8

Familiarize yourself with fire safety procedures and follow all regulations.

Rule No.9

Dormitories typically have strict policies regarding alcohol and drug use.

Rule No.10

Inform your roommates and resident director if you plan to have guests over.
